How IFS Therapy Helps You Stop Repeating the Same Relationship Pattern

← Back to the Blog
Rose Andre
Published March 24, 2026 · IFS Therapy

At some point after your second, third, or fifth breakup that follows a strangely familiar shape, you start to notice it: different person, same pattern. Maybe it's always someone emotionally unavailable. Maybe it's always you shrinking yourself to keep the peace. Naming the pattern is a useful first step, but naming it rarely stops it. That takes different work.

Why Insight Alone Doesn't Change the Pattern

Most people who repeat a relationship pattern already know they're doing it. They can describe it accurately, sometimes in detail, in a first therapy session. And yet the next relationship often looks the same anyway. That's because the pattern isn't a belief you can simply reason your way out of — it's a part of you running a strategy it learned a long time ago, usually well before adulthood, and that part doesn't update just because your conscious mind understands the pattern intellectually.

What's Actually Running the Pattern

In IFS terms, there's usually a part of you attached to a specific relational role — the one who fixes people, the one who stays small to avoid conflict, the one who chases anyone who seems slightly unavailable. That part formed for a reason, often in response to an early relationship where that strategy genuinely kept you safer or more loved. The problem isn't that the part is wrong. It's that the part hasn't caught up to the fact that you don't need that exact strategy anymore.

How the Work Actually Changes Things

Instead of just pointing out the pattern, IFS work goes to the part running it: when did this start, what was it protecting you from, and what does it need to feel safe trying something different. As that part builds trust with your core Self, it gradually loosens its grip on the old strategy — not because you forced it to, but because it finally believes there's a safer way to get what it actually wants.

If you can already describe your pattern but can't seem to stop living it, that's a sign the work needs to go deeper than insight — which is exactly where IFS therapy is built to go.
