How to Handle Seeing Your Ex Around Salt Lake City After a Breakup

← Back to the Blog
Rose Andre
Published February 12, 2026 · Local & Practical

One of the things that makes breakups in the Salt Lake Valley uniquely hard is how connected everything feels. Shared friend groups, shared gyms, shared hiking trails, the same three coffee shops in Murray or Sugar House everyone seems to cycle through. Unlike a breakup where you can simply avoid a whole city, here you're often planning your life around the possibility of running into someone.

Why This Hits Differently

Every unexpected sighting — at Trader Joe's, at a mutual friend's barbecue, in the parking lot of your own gym — sends a jolt through your nervous system. It's not just awkward; it's a genuine stress response, the same fight-or-flight activation you'd get from any perceived threat, because on some level, seeing them still registers as danger to the part of you that's trying to protect your heart.

Practical Strategies That Actually Help

You can't control the size of the valley, but you can control a few things: having a simple exit plan for shared spaces (a friend you can call, a general strategy of a polite nod and moving on rather than a full conversation), giving yourself permission to change your routine for a while without guilt, and preparing a few neutral lines in advance so you're not scrambling for words in the moment.

The Deeper Work

The goal isn't to never feel anything when you see them — it's to get to a place where seeing them doesn't undo weeks of progress. That's less about avoidance and more about building enough internal stability that an unexpected run-in is uncomfortable, not devastating. That stability is exactly what we build in therapy: understanding which part of you panics at the sight of them, and helping that part trust that you can handle it now.

If the Salt Lake Valley's small-world feeling is making your breakup harder to move through, that's a completely valid thing to bring into a session.
