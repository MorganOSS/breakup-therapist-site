Virtual vs. In-Person Therapy After a Breakup: What Works Best in Utah

← Back to the Blog
Rose Andre
Published February 20, 2026 · Local & Practical

Utah's geography makes this question more practical than philosophical for a lot of people. If you're in Murray or close to the Salt Lake Valley's core, an in-person session might be a fifteen-minute drive. If you're further out — Tooele, Park City, rural Utah County — virtual sessions might be the only realistic option. But even when both are available, the choice matters more than people expect.

What In-Person Offers

There's something about physically leaving your house, sitting in a different room, and being fully present without a screen between you and your therapist that changes the texture of a session. In-person work tends to suit people who are newly out of a breakup and need the physical ritual of "going somewhere to process this" — it creates a container the emotional work can live inside.

What Virtual Offers

Virtual sessions remove the barrier of a commute, which matters more than people admit, especially in the early weeks after a breakup when getting out of bed and driving anywhere can feel like its own accomplishment. Virtual also tends to suit people juggling shift work, single parenting, or a schedule that doesn't allow for a drive to Murray mid-week.

How to Actually Decide

Ask yourself honestly: do I need the physical separation of a dedicated space to actually open up, or would the friction of getting there stop me from going at all? Neither answer is wrong. Some clients start in-person and move to virtual once they're stabilized; others do the opposite, starting virtual because it's the only way they'll start at all, then moving in-person once they want more.

I offer both, and we can always adjust as your needs change — this isn't a decision you have to get right on the first try.
