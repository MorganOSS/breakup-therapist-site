Grief

This grief deserves real space

Losing a relationship is a legitimate loss — of a person, a future, a version of yourself — even though there's no funeral and few people checking in on you a month later.

People around you may expect you to be "over it" faster than feels possible, especially if the relationship ended by choice, or wasn't a marriage, or lasted "only" a few months. None of that makes the loss less real.

This work makes room for that grief fully, without rushing you toward "moving on" before you're ready.

Who This Is For

You might be here because —

  • You're grieving a relationship ending, even if others don't see it as a "big" loss
  • You feel grief that comes in waves — fine one day, flattened the next
  • You're mourning not just a person, but a future you'd planned around them
  • You want space to actually feel this, not just strategies to get over it faster
What Sessions Look Like

We won't rush the timeline. Grief isn't linear, and neither is this work — some weeks are about the practical rebuilding, others are just about sitting with how much this hurts.

Over time, we make room for the loss without letting it be the only thing that defines you right now.

Quick Answers

Common questions about this service.

Is it normal to grieve a relationship that wasn't that long?

Yes. Grief is about what the relationship meant to you and the future you'd imagined — not how long it lasted on a calendar.

What if people keep telling me to just move on?

That's common, and it's not helpful. This space is built to let you grieve at your actual pace, not everyone else's expectations of it.

You don't have to have it figured out to reach out.
