Anxious Attachment & Relationship Patterns

If you keep ending up here — anxious, overgiving, afraid of being left — this is where we start tracing it back.

Patterns

The anxiety has a root, and it's not "too much"

If you find yourself anxious in relationships — overanalyzing texts, fearing abandonment, losing yourself to keep someone close — we'll trace that pattern to where it started, usually long before this relationship.

Understanding your attachment style isn't about diagnosing yourself or finding a label to blame. It's about giving you real choice in how you show up next time — instead of the same reflexive fear driving the bus.

Who This Is For

You might be here because —

  • You notice the same anxious patterns across multiple relationships, not just this one
  • You often feel "too much" in relationships, or brace for people to leave
  • You find yourself overgiving, over-apologizing, or shrinking to keep the peace
  • You want to understand the why behind the anxiety, not just manage the symptoms
What Sessions Look Like

We'll look at where these patterns first made sense — often in early relationships, family dynamics, or past heartbreak — and untangle what's an old survival strategy from what's actually true about you and your worth.

Alongside that, we'll build real, practical tools for the moments the anxiety spikes: the unanswered text, the fear of being too much, the urge to chase reassurance.

Quick Answers

Common questions about this service.

Is anxious attachment something that can actually change?

Yes. Attachment styles are patterns, not permanent traits — they formed for a reason and they can shift with the right support and enough repeated, different experiences.

Do I need to be in a relationship right now to do this work?

No. A lot of this work happens between relationships, when there's more room to look at the pattern without being in the middle of it.

You don't have to have it figured out to reach out.
