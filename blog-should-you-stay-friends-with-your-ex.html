Should You Stay Friends With Your Ex? A Therapist's Take

← Back to the Blog
Rose Andre
Published June 20, 2026 · Breakup Psychology

"Can you really stay friends with an ex?" gets debated endlessly, usually with people arguing for a universal rule in either direction. In practice, the answer depends heavily on specifics that a blanket rule can't account for.

Why Timing Matters More Than the Rule Itself

Attempting friendship immediately after a breakup, before either person has processed the loss, tends to keep both people entangled in a way that delays healing for at least one of you, often both. Friendship that's attempted too early frequently functions as a way to avoid the full grief of the ending, rather than a genuine, healthy connection.

Questions Worth Asking Honestly

Do you actually want their friendship, or do you want to stay close enough that the door to reconciliation stays open? Can you tolerate hearing about their new relationships without it derailing you? Is there a specific, practical reason for ongoing contact (kids, shared business, mutual community) or is it more about not being ready to fully let go?

When Friendship Tends to Work

Post-breakup friendship tends to work best when both people have had real space first, when both are genuinely over the romantic attachment rather than performing that they are, and when the friendship is built on its own terms rather than being a downgraded version of the relationship you already had.

When It Tends to Backfire

It backfires most often when one person still holds romantic hope, when the "friendship" mostly consists of the same emotional intimacy minus the label, or when it's motivated by fear of fully losing the person rather than a genuine desire for platonic connection.

There's No Shame in Either Choice

Choosing not to stay friends isn't cold, and choosing to stay friends isn't naive — both are legitimate outcomes depending on your specific situation. What matters is being honest with yourself about which motivation is actually driving the decision.
